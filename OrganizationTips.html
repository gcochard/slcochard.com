Stay Organized, Stick to Your Resolutions

Clutter is like kryptonite to New Year’s Resolutions. The last thing you need derailing your new-found good habits are extraneous papers piling up on your yoga mat or fatty foods clogging up your refrigerator. Getting your life organized can help you stick to your resolutions by eliminating old routines that prevent you from reaching your goals. After all, you don’t want that new exercise machine to become a decorative statue in your living room. No matter what your goals are, these tips can help you get organized for a successful year:

Pick One Goal – Pick one area of your life and start there. Do you want to begin by organizing your finances, diet, or exercise routine? Once you determine a topic of focus you’ll be better suited to follow through with your commitments. You might not be able to do everything, but you can do one thing well.

Work in Small Sections - Don’t expect a transformation overnight. Start small and work your way up.

  • Home Organization: Pick one room and organize it completely. Try not to move items to other rooms unless they are completely necessary. Donate any unwanted materials that are still in good condition before moving on to another room to organize.
  • Finances: Start by tracking your spending, as this activity will likely influence other areas like savings and investments. Once you have a handle on spending, move on to another fiscal area.
  • Exercise: Clear the path to your exercise routine. Start by making the effort to put fitness information, workout clothes, shoes, and equipment in plain view so it becomes a priority in your life. 
  • Diet: Begin by cleaning out your refrigerator and pantry, eliminating unhealthy foods you’d like to avoid in the New Year. Once you have a clean slate, stock only healthy foods that will help you stick to your health goals.  

Create a Calendar – Regardless of the topic, scheduling time on a calendar to focus on your goal will help you achieve it. Make time to check in with yourself weekly to measure your progress.

Reward Yourself – All work and no play may make you dull and irritable. Did you drop a few pounds because of your new diet? Buy yourself a new outfit to show it off. Have you been good at saving your money? Give yourself a pat on the back in the form of a nice piece of cake. Bearing in mind that you are human and deserve a break will go a long way to keep you on target.
