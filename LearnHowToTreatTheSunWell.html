Summer’s Here, So Treat the Sun Well

It just takes one. That one memory of that one sunburn so awful, unforgettable, and uncomfortable, that the pain you experienced will bake into your memory and never let you forget the importance of sun protection. Chances are we’ve all forgotten to re-apply sunscreen after staying out in the sun’s rays too long, only to pay the price later when trying to do simple activities like sit or bend our arms. (When someone says you’re knocking that robot impression out of the park you know you’ve baked too long.)

As hard as we may try, sometimes we lose the boiling battle and end up winning first prize in a lobster lookalike contest (all the pain without the benefits of butter for dipping).  

Since accidents do happen when we are too busy catching the best wave to reapply sunscreen, learn to treat sunburns well. Understanding steps you can take to reduce your discomfort and ease the healing period can make the difference in the first 48 hours when the heat and pain is at its worst. Try these over-the-counter home remedies approved by the CDC:

  • Nonprescription 1.0% hydrocortisone cream, aloe vera or regular moisturizing creams may be applied three times a day to cut down on swelling, pain and itchiness.

  • Cool baths and cool, wet compresses relieve pain. To ease discomfort, you might want to add two ounces of baking soda or an oatmeal-based bath product to bath water. Drink plenty of fluids the first two days after getting sunburned to prevent dizziness or dehydration.

  • Consult your doctor about how to care for broken blisters. Do not break existing blisters; see a doctor immediately if you have any concerns.

  • Peeling usually occurs in about one week. Apply aloe vera or a moisturizing cream and do not peel off dead skin before the skin underneath is completely healed.

  • Wear loose fitting clothing to reduce friction on the skin highly sensitive to touch.

Things to avoid: Avoid applying petroleum jelly, butter or other oil products to sunburned areas. These prevent heat and sweat from escaping and are painful to remove. Also avoid applying first-aid sprays or creams that contain benzocaine. These might cause an allergic rash, which can complicate your symptoms.

Don’t let a sunburn crash your summer block party. Be smart but be prepared for the occasional sunscreen slip-up and lather these UV treatments into your mind to neutralize any threats to beach bliss.
